Reading Reminders before Testing

First Read

  1. Read the title to get an idea of what the text will be about. Is it historical? Literature? Science or geography? A story? Knowing what the text is about helps you prepare and understand it better.
  2. Read the text through once, pausing where there are commas and stopping when you see a period. Reading with punctuation helps you understand the text better.
  3. Check for words you don't understand and try to figure out their meaning by the words and explanations around them (context clues).

The Question

  1. Read the question carefully and try to figure out what type of question it is. Cause and effect? Chronological order? Main idea? Supporting detail? Knowing this helps you look for specific words and choose an answer that really answers the question.
  2. Find the key words in the question that you can read or skim for in the text to support your answer.

The Answers

  1. Carefully read every answer and — in your head — cross out the ones that you are absolutely sure are incorrect.
  2. Make sure the answer includes key words depending on the type of question. For example: if it's chronological order, check for time words (first/then/before, etc.); if it's a cause and effect question, look for result words such as because/why/in order to, etc.
  3. If you have doubts, don't commit to an answer without checking the text again.

The Proof

  1. Go back to the text and find evidence that supports the answer, directly or indirectly.
  2. Don't guess!

One more thing…

Don't forget to read the explanations after answering! Even if you got it right, these explanations will give you hints and ideas on how to answer these types of questions correctly in the future.
